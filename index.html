About Me

Though I didn't know it at the time, I began programming when I was 12, copying and pasting snippets of ActionScript toghether to make flash games. It took Tufts' COMP11 class for me to rediscover my love for solving problems with technology. Now almost a decade after I wrote my first script, I've found myself in love with solving programs anywhere in the development stack, with a particular affinity for working on the backend of web stacks.

I helped work on iZotope's Iris 2 (look for me in the promo video!) as part of my iZotope internship in 2014. Recently, I've worked on a few closed-source projects and websites for clients as a freelance developer. You can see some projects I've worked on below, or see code that I've open-sourced at my GitHub.
